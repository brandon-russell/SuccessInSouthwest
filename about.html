About Chris

Chris Boso for South Western School Board

Proven Leadership. Real Results. A Future-Focused Vision.

Born and raised in Grove City, Chris Boso is a proud graduate of Grove City High School who has dedicated his life to serving the South Western community. Alongside his wife, Shelly, he raised three children—all graduates of South Western City Schools. That personal connection fuels his deep commitment to providing every student with a high-quality education and meaningful opportunities for success.

A Leader Who Delivers

Over the past four years, Chris has served on the South Western School Board and currently holds the position of Board President. Under his leadership, the district has experienced a positive shift—marked by:

  • Academic progress
  • Stronger accountability
  • A renewed focus on core educational values

As both a Columbus Firefighter and local small business owner, Chris brings hands-on experience, practical problem-solving, and service-driven leadership to the board.

Grounded in Faith and Community

Chris's leadership reflects the values that guide his life:

  • Active church involvement
  • Regular community volunteerism
  • A firm belief in service, integrity, and the power of local voices

He prioritizes students first, supports parental rights, and ensures teachers and staff are heard and supported.

Accomplishments on the School Board

  • Restricted cell phone use in classrooms— before it became a statewide issue
  • Mandated all board meetings be recorded and posted online for transparency
  • Reinstated the Pledge of Allegiance and placed American flags in every classroom
  • Expanded trade and career programs after seeing their impact firsthand through his son’s experience
  • Regular school visits to engage with staff, listen to concerns, and take action

Chris also makes it a priority to connect with parents and taxpayers across the district, serving as both a voice and a listener at the board table. Their input, combined with staff feedback, has helped shape goals for the coming year:

  • Improving student behavior
  • Protecting the integrity of the learning environment

Why He’s Running Again

Chris Boso is seeking re-election to continue the progress and strengthen South Western as a district families are proud to call home. His vision is focused on:

  • Expanding educational opportunities
  • Preparing students with life skills, career readiness, and character development
  • Staying rooted in traditional values that uplift our community
“Let’s keep the success going. Re-elect Chris Boso for South Western School Board.”

About Steve

Steve Feucht for South Western School Board

Experience. Integrity. A Vision for the Future.

Raised in Grove City, Steve Feucht is a 1982 graduate of Grove City High School and a lifelong resident with deep roots in the community. Married to his wife, Ann, for 40 years, he is a proud father and grandfather who is passionate about building a strong, thriving future for the next generation.

Real-World Experience, Proven Leadership

For over four decades, Steve has successfully owned and operated a small business while also working as a consultant for an international firm. With unmatched expertise in finance and a sharp eye for identifying creative solutions within complex systems, he brings:

  • Real-world business experience
  • Strategic, financial insight
  • A reputation as a practical problem-solver
  • A collaborative, team-first mindset

Steve believes that meaningful progress is built on teamwork—and he brings that spirit to every challenge he faces.

Guided by Faith, Committed to Service

A man of faith, Steve lives out his values by serving others. His dedication to helping families, mentoring youth, and standing up for what's right reflects the integrity and character he brings to leadership roles both in and out of the public eye.

A Voice for Students and Families

When the 2008 school levy failed, Steve showed up and stood strong—attending every school board meeting for two years to fight for the restoration of extracurricular activities and the arts. His advocacy didn’t stop there:

  • Active supporter of youth sports programs
  • Hands-on help for families in need throughout the community
  • Focused on preserving and enriching educational opportunities for all

Why He’s Running

Now, Steve is ready to bring his financial expertise, business insight, and collaborative approach to the school board. His mission is simple but powerful:

  • Create a district that thrives, not one that just gets by
  • Build a place where families want to stay
  • Inspire students to succeed
  • Ensure future generations are proud to call South Western home
“Together, let’s build a brighter future—one where ALL South Western City Schools lead the way.”

About Jason

Rooted in Community. Driven by Purpose. Committed to Students.

A proud 1996 graduate of Franklin Heights High School and lifelong resident of the South Western City School District, Jason Gocha is deeply committed to the success of our students, schools, and community. Married for 18 years and raising three children who currently attend South Western schools, Jason brings both personal investment and a strong sense of purpose to his campaign for school board.

Experienced, Practical, and Ready to Lead

Professionally, Jason serves as an operational manager—a role that demands:

  • Strategic planning
  • Team leadership
  • Effective problem-solving

With a background in operations and skilled trades, Jason offers a practical understanding of resource management and real-world solutions—experience that equips him to make thoughtful, informed decisions as a member of the school board.

A Champion for Students and Families

Jason and his wife have actively served in PTAs across multiple schools in the district. He has:

  • Coached numerous youth sports teams
  • Served as Vice President of the Grove City High School Marching Band Boosters
  • Supported the arts and expanded extracurricular programming

He understands the transformative power of music, sports, and clubs in shaping students’ confidence, creativity, and connection to school.

Rooted in Faith, Focused on Inclusion

A man of faith and an active member of his church, Jason believes in giving back and fostering a culture of teamwork and mutual respect. He is especially passionate about supporting all learners, including those with:

  • Individualized Education Programs (IEPs)
  • 504 Plans

He is committed to ensuring that every student has an advocate at the table and a real opportunity to thrive.

Jason’s Vision

Jason Gocha believes in creating a school environment where every student can grow both academically and personally. His top priorities include:

  • Minimizing distractions in the classroom
  • Prioritizing student and staff safety
  • Expanding access to quality education—inside and outside the classroom

Why He’s Running

With deep roots in the community and a proven commitment to service, Jason Gocha isn’t just running to make a difference— he’s running because he genuinely cares about every student, staff member, and family in the district. He’s ready to:

  • Listen
  • Collaborate
  • Work hard to help South Western City Schools grow stronger
“Let’s create a future where every child has the tools to succeed—today and tomorrow.”
